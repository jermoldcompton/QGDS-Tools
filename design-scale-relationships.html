Design scale relationships

Grid, Size and Spacing scales share an exponential structure with calibrated divergence rates, each tuned to the perceptual demands of its role.

Scale comparison

Grid Size Spacing
Grid scale grows slowly as the common denominator; Size and Spacing diverge progressively faster.
Export chart
Size and Spacing both start at 2× the Grid increment (a=4 vs a=2) and pull further ahead as their faster growth intervals accumulate. All three scales share the same stepped exponential structure, diverging by design, not by accident.
Each scale's step size stays flat, then doubles at its interval, every 24 steps for Grid, 12 for Size, 8 for Spacing. The doubling intervals are in the ratio 3 : 2 : 1.

Element sizing
Size

Doubles twice as fast as Grid. UI elements require visually distinct steps, as small increments at large values are imperceptible.
Spatial hierarchy
Spacing
S=0, a=4, b=2, c=8
Doubles three times as fast as Grid. Spatial relationships require strong contrast to communicate hierarchy across their range.
